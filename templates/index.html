Machine Unlearning

RPU — Representation-Prototype-guided Unlearning

Make a trained model forget one class — as cleanly as a model retrained from scratch on the remaining data.

The problem

  • Models memorise. Deployed classifiers carry the influence of every training sample.
  • Naive deletion leaks. Forcing forget-class accuracy to zero still leaves a contaminated output distribution — exposed by membership-inference and reminiscence audits.
  • Retraining is too slow. The gold-standard fix takes hours and isn't tenable for compliance turnaround.

Novelty

  • Contamination-aware target. Per-sample zero-forget-mass distribution, built once before fine-tuning.
  • Two evidence streams. Fuses the original output's retained-class preference with retained-class prototype geometry in the representation space.
  • Adaptive contamination defence. The fusion reduces reliance on the original posterior when it is forget-dominated or weakly supported by retained structure — closer to the retrain reference without paying the retrain cost.

1 · Output evidence

What does M0's output say after we delete the forgotten classes?

  • Retained-side anchor t(·|x) — take the original probabilities P0(·|x), drop the forgotten classes, renormalise the rest. Preserves fine-grained retained-class preferences but inherits any contamination from the forgotten classes that were competing in the output.
  • Forgotten mass mf(x) — how much probability the original model placed on the forgotten classes. A larger mf means the anchor was renormalised from less retained mass — less trustworthy.

2 · Structural evidence

Where does x sit among the retained classes in the model's representation space?

  • Retained prototypes μc(ℓ) — per-layer, per-class mean directions in M0's features. Cached once, frozen throughout unlearning.
  • Hierarchical structural posterior aH(·|x) — for every layer, softmax x's similarity to each retained prototype, then KL-geometrically fuse the layers, weighting each by global separation Sℓ + local affinity Uℓ(x).
  • Structural validity V(x) — whether the layers driving the fusion also show positive retained-prototype affinity for x. Gates how much to trust the structural branch.

Why RTD matters

Standard accuracy can be fooled: every well-tuned method drives forget-test accuracy to near 0. RTD measures how closely the unlearned model's full output distribution on forget-class inputs matches a retrain reference (averaged over 10 retrains). RTD = 1 is exact retrain behavior; values near 0 or negative mean no improvement or movement away from the retrain reference.

RPU is the only method that lands close to the retrain reference on this audit.
