Machine Unlearning

RPU — Representation-Prototype-guided Unlearning

Teach a trained model to forget a class — as cleanly as if it were never trained on it.

The problem

Modern AI models memorise the training data they see. Privacy laws and compliance frameworks now demand a way to remove the influence of specific data from a deployed model without paying the cost of retraining from scratch.

Why simple suppression isn't enough

Many existing methods just push the forget-class accuracy to zero. The model's internal representations still carry signals about the forgotten data — leakage that membership-inference attacks and reminiscence audits can detect.

The RPU idea

RPU extracts per-class prototypes from the model's hidden layers and builds a contamination-aware target for every forget-class sample. It fuses two pieces of evidence — the original model's retained-class probabilities and the structural similarity to each retained prototype — then fine-tunes the model against these targets for only a few epochs.

I

Prototype Extraction & Target Construction

Compute class-mean directions from retained-class representations. For each forget sample, fuse the original model's retained-side probabilities with structural prototype evidence into a clean zero-forget-mass target.

one-time, frozen
↓
II

Fine-tune the Model

Optimise retained-class cross-entropy + KL divergence between the forget-sample outputs and the pre-computed targets. The model converges in minutes, not hours.

few epochs
